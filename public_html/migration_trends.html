How to use this chart

How to use it

This chart starts with a curated subset of species already selected — Red-tailed Hawk, Sharp-shinned Hawk, Broad-winged Hawk, Merlin, and Turkey Vulture, the five most-counted raptors at this site — rather than all ~26 at once. That's expected, not a bug; use the legend on the right to add or remove species.

Press Play to animate the count through each fall migration season from 2000 to 2025, one season at a time. Use step to move one season at a time, or drag the scrubber handle to jump anywhere. Click directly on a year along the scrubber to pause and reveal that season's Aug–Nov monthly breakdown.

Counts span four orders of magnitude between species, so the chart defaults to a log scale — this keeps rare species like Golden Eagle or Gyrfalcon visible as real trends instead of flat lines near zero. Switch to Linear above the chart to compare true-to-scale magnitudes instead.

In the legend, hover a species name to see its photo, and click the name to open its full species profile — except for the Unknown/Other codes and four species (Osprey, Golden Eagle, Merlin, Peregrine Falcon) that don't yet have a profile in this dataset; those still plot with real counts, just without a click-through.

About this data

These counts come from the Illinois Beach State Park Hawk Watch, on the Lake Michigan shoreline in Lake County, Illinois — an all-volunteer effort that has run every fall season since 2000.

In 1999, Vic Berardi scouted sites across northeastern Illinois for a viable raptor migration count and settled on a spot within the state park. The following year he conducted the first season with Janice Sweet, Paul Sweet, Bill Wengelewski, and others — every year since has brought more volunteers.
