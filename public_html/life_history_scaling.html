    About this page

    How to use it

    The dataset loads automatically once you close this panel, so choose which life-history trait to compare against body mass in Compare body mass against. Each dot is a species, plotted on a log mass scale; the shaded band and line trace the median and interquartile range across the dataset.

    Drag across the chart to brush a mass range and list the species in it. Hovering a dot shows its name — to find a specific species, type its common or scientific name into the search box beside the chart; picking a match highlights its dot with a ring and opens a detail card below the search box with its mass, chosen trait value, and conservation status. Click a dot, or the photo/name in its detail card, to open the full species profile. Switch Color by to highlight conservation status.

    About this data

    The species data on this page comes from BIRDBASE, a peer-reviewed global dataset of avian biogeography, conservation, ecology, and life-history traits — 78 traits for 11,589 species across 254 families, synthesized from 367 published sources spanning 1957–2025.

    Şekercioğlu, Ç.H., Kittelberger, K.D., Mota, F.M.M. et al. BIRDBASE: A Global Dataset of Avian Biogeography, Conservation, Ecology and Life History Traits. Scientific Data (2025).

    This page is a downstream retelling of that dataset, not a replacement for the source — see the full paper on nature.com for methodology, sources, and citation details.
